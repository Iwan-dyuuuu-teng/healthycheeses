Italy's hard-aged wonder — nutrient-dense and deeply flavorful.

Health Benefits
One ounce of Parmesan delivers about 335 mg of calcium — roughly 30% of your daily needs. Calcium is essential for bone density, nerve signaling, and muscle contraction.
The long aging process (12–36 months) breaks down virtually all lactose, making Parmesan one of the safest cheeses for lactose-intolerant people.
With ~10 g of protein per ounce, Parmesan contains all essential amino acids. Its protein bioavailability is excellent, supporting muscle repair and satiety.
Parmesan provides meaningful amounts of phosphorus (for bone health) and zinc (for immune function and wound healing), making it a surprisingly mineral-dense food.

Health Benefits
Ricotta is made from whey — the liquid left after other cheeses are formed. Whey protein is rapidly absorbed, making ricotta ideal post-workout for muscle synthesis and recovery.
Ricotta contains riboflavin (B2) and vitamin A, both linked to reduced risk of cataracts and age-related macular degeneration. It's a rare cheese with meaningful vitamin A content.
Part-skim ricotta has a lower saturated fat content than most cheeses. Studies suggest moderate dairy consumption, particularly whey-rich varieties, may support healthy cholesterol levels.
Ricotta offers a solid amount of selenium, an antioxidant mineral that supports thyroid function, DNA synthesis, and may help protect cells from oxidative damage.

Health Benefits
Cottage cheese is predominantly casein, which digests slowly and provides a sustained release of amino acids. This makes it popular as a night-time snack to support overnight muscle maintenance.
With a very high protein-to-calorie ratio, cottage cheese promotes satiety without excess energy intake. Research has linked regular consumption to better weight control and reduced body fat percentage.
Live-culture cottage cheese varieties contain probiotics that promote a healthy gut microbiome, improve digestion, and may boost immune response.
Cottage cheese has a low glycemic index. Its combination of protein and fat slows glucose absorption, making it a smart choice for people managing blood sugar or following a lower-carb diet.

Health Benefits
Feta has fewer calories per ounce than cheddar, gouda, or brie, yet its strong flavor means a little goes a long way. You get big taste with less caloric cost.
Traditional feta is made with Lactobacillus plantarum and other probiotic bacteria. These strains are associated with reduced inflammation, improved immunity, and better gut flora balance.
Feta provides calcium and phosphorus in a ratio that promotes bone mineralization. It also contains vitamin K2, which is crucial for directing calcium into bones rather than arteries.
Feta made from sheep's milk is high in CLA, a fatty acid linked to reduced body fat, improved insulin sensitivity, and potential anti-cancer properties in research studies.
